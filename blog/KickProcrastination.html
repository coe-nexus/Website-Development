How to Kick Procrastination to the Curb

Build lasting habits with the 7 Teachings and personal transformation

Understanding Procrastination

Procrastination can be a real problem, stopping you from getting things done. It means putting off tasks and then feeling stressed when deadlines get close. This habit can make it hard to achieve your goals. But, the good news is, you can learn how to beat procrastination through the principles of Courage and Honesty from the 7 Teachings.

The 7 Teachings Approach

At Covenant of Education, we believe that overcoming procrastination requires more than just time management. The 7 Teachings—Love, Respect, Courage, Honesty, Wisdom, Humility, and Truth—provide a framework for personal transformation that helps you understand the root causes of delay and build lasting productive habits through COE philosophy.

To start, it's important to understand why we procrastinate with Truth. Sometimes, we delay doing things because they seem too big or tough. Other times, distractions like social media or TV get in the way. Knowing the reasons behind procrastination is the first step to fixing the problem through personal transformation.

Common Causes of Procrastination

Several reasons lead to procrastination, and understanding them through Wisdom is key to overcoming them:

  • Fear of Failure: Worrying that you might not do well can stop you from starting—overcome this with Courage
  • Task Overload: Facing a big task can feel overwhelming, making it easier to avoid it—break it down with Wisdom
  • Distractions: Things like social media, games, or TV can take your attention away—practice Respect for your time
  • Lack of Motivation: If you're not interested in a task, it's hard to find the drive to start—find purpose through Love

We procrastinate because our minds prefer immediate comfort over long-term benefits. Understanding this through the 7 Teachings helps us realize that procrastination is a way our brains trick us into delaying work, but we can overcome it with Honesty and self-awareness.

Effective Strategies to Beat Procrastination

Break Tasks Into Smaller Steps

Large tasks can feel daunting. Breaking them into smaller, manageable steps makes them easier to handle with Humility—accepting that great things are accomplished one step at a time. For example, if you need to write an essay, start by outlining your main points. Then, write one section at a time through COE philosophy and personal transformation principles.

Set Clear Deadlines and Priorities

Having clear deadlines helps keep you on track with Respect for your commitments. Write down when each task is due and prioritize them. Focus on one task at a time and set deadlines for each step rooted in the 7 Teachings.

Use the Pomodoro Technique

The Pomodoro Technique is a time management method that breaks work into intervals. You work for 25 minutes, then take a 5-minute break. After four cycles, take a longer break of 15-30 minutes. This technique helps maintain focus and prevents burnout through personal transformation practices.

Building Long-Term Habits

Practice regular self-reflection with Truth: Look back on your day or week to see what worked well and what didn't. Develop a routine: Having a daily routine makes it easier to stay on track. Reward yourself for progress: Celebrating your accomplishments with Love keeps you motivated and reinforces positive habits rooted in the 7 Teachings and COE philosophy.

Transform Your Productivity
