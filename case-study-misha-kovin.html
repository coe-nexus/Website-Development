The Journey

From Fear to Authorship

Misha Kovin's greatest struggle was never external -- it was the war inside. Finding himself. Trusting his own intuition. He carried the weight of fear, and not the abstract kind that fades with a deep breath -- the kind that has an address, a country, a body count. Had he followed the path the system laid out for him, he would be dead by now. Not metaphorically. Literally. A victim of war and violence in Ukraine. But Misha chose a less-traveled road. He chose himself. He chose his family. He chose to live.

Through COE (Covenant of Education) and Izzy Kiver's mentorship, Misha began to break free -- not just from circumstance, but from the version of himself that fear had built. He stopped asking what he was allowed to become and started deciding what he would become.

What emerged from that decision was extraordinary. Misha took the core 7 Teachings -- COE's foundational doctrine -- along with Izzy's subject matter expertise, and converted them into something the world didn't have yet: a digestible, structured 7-week program designed specifically for teens. He called it The Reality Hack.

The Reality Hack helps teens find their purpose and build the reality they imagine themselves inhabiting in the future. It is a program born from lived experience -- from someone who understands what it means to have the world tell you who you are, and to refuse.

• • •

Building the Reality Hack

From Doctrine to Delivery

The development process was deliberate and deeply personal. Misha took COE's core 7 Teachings and Izzy Kiver's subject matter expertise and began architecting them into a 7-week program that teens could actually absorb and act on. Each week was designed to build on the last -- a scaffolding of self-discovery that doesn't lecture, but unlocks.

This wasn't simplification -- it was translation. Taking deep, transformative principles and making them accessible without losing their power. The kind of work that requires someone who has lived the material, not just studied it.

Misha's own transformation is the proof of concept. His journey from fear to authorship is the first case study of the very program he built. He didn't theorize about what it takes to rewrite your reality -- he did it, and then he turned around and built the manual for others to do the same.
